About

I grew up in Venezuela — a country where I watched institutions fail, democracy erode, and technology become a tool of control rather than liberation. That experience didn't make me cynical. It made me determined.

Today I study Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, where I'm building the technical foundation I'll need to work at the intersection of cybersecurity, law, and democratic governance. I'm not interested in technology for its own sake. I'm interested in what technology does to power — and what law can do to protect people from its abuses.

"The most dangerous systems are the ones no one thought to regulate until it was too late."

My path leads toward law school, with a focus on technology law, election security, privacy rights, and AI governance. Long-term, I want to hold the kind of public leadership role that can actually change how democratic institutions are designed and protected — in the United States and across Latin America.

I came here as an immigrant. I'm building toward becoming a leader. The distance between those two points is where I do my work.
